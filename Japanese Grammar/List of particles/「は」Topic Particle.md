- Identifies what it is that you’re talking about, essentially the topic of your sentence
- Pronounced as /wa/
## Example 1
Once topic is established, the responder don't need to repeat the topic

ボブ：アリス==は==学生？  
Bob: Is Alice (you) student?

アリス：うん、学生。  
Alice: Yeah, (I) am. 
## Example 2
Without context the meaning sometimes couldn't be extracted

ボブ：ジョン==は==明日？
Bob: John is tomorrow?

アリス：ううん、明日じゃない。  
Alice: No, not tomorrow.
## Example 3
Topic can be referring to any action or object from anywhere even including other sentences

アリス：今日は試験だ。
Alice: Today is exam.

ボブ：ジョンは？
Bob: What about John?

アリス：ジョンは明日。 
Alice: John is tomorrow. (As for John, the exam is tomorrow.)
